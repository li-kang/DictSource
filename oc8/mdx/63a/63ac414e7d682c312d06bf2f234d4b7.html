pre·modi·fier / ˌpriːˈmɒdɪfaɪə(r) ; NAmE ˌpriːˈmɑːdɪfaɪər / noun ( grammar 语法 ) a word, especially an adjective or a noun, that is placed before a noun and describes it or restricts its meaning in some way 前置修饰语；前修饰成分 ◆ In ‘a loud noise’, the adjective ‘loud’ is a premodifier. 在 a loud noise 中，形容词 loud 是前置修饰语。 ☞compare modifier , postmodifier pre·modi·fier / ˌpriːˈmɒdɪfaɪə(r) ; NAmE ˌpriːˈmɑːdɪfaɪər /
