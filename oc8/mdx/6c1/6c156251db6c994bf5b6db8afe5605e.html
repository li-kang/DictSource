fever ★ / ˈfiːvə(r) ; NAmE ˈfiːvər / noun 1 ★ [countable ,  uncountable ] a medical condition in which a person has a temperature that is higher than normal 发烧；发热 ◆ He has a high fever. 他发高烧。 ◆ Aspirin should help reduce the fever. 阿司匹林能够退烧。 ☞ collocationsat ill ☞compare temperature  (2 ) 2 ★ [countable ,  uncountable ] ( old-fashioned) ( used mainly in compounds 主要用于构成复合词 ) a particular type of disease in which sb has a high temperature 热（病） ◆ She caught a fever on her travels in Africa, and died. 她在非洲旅行时患热病而死。 ☞see also glandular fever , hay fever , rheumatic fever , scarlet fever , yellow fever 3 [singular ] fever(of sth) a state of nervous excitement 激动不安；兴奋紧张 ◆ He waited for her arrival in a fever of impatience. 他焦急不安地等待她的到来。 4 [uncountable ] ( especially in compounds 尤用于构成复合词 ) great interest or excitement about sth 狂热 ◆ election fever 选举热 fever fevers fevered fevering fever / ˈfiːvə(r) ; NAmE ˈfiːvər /
