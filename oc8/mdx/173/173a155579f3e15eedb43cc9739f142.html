Pyr·rhic vic·tory / ˌpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri ; NAmE ˌpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri / noun a victory that is not worth winning because the winner has suffered or lost so much in winning it 得不偿失的胜利；以惨重代价换取的胜利 ORIGIN From Pyrrhus,the king of Epirus who defeated the Romans in 279BC but lost many of his own men. 源自伊庇鲁斯国王皮洛士，他于公元前 279 年打败罗马人，但自己的部队也伤亡惨重。 Pyr·rhic vic·tory / ˌpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri ; NAmE ˌpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri /
