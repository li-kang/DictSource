to promise sth will happen 保证；担保；保障 guaranteesth ◆ Basic human rights, including freedom of speech, are now guaranteed. 现在，包括言论自由在内的基本人权已有了保障。 guarantee(that)… ◆ We cannot guarantee (that) our flights will never be delayed. 我们不能保证我们的所有航班均不误点。 guaranteesb sth ◆ The ticket will guarantee you free entry. 这张票可保证你免费入场。 guaranteeto do sth ◆ We guarantee to deliver your goods within a week. 我们保证一周内交货。 2 ★ guaranteesth (against sth) to give a written promise to replace or repair a product free if it goes wrong 出具保单（免费掉换或修理有问题的产品） ◆ This iron is guaranteed for a year against faulty workmanship. 这种熨斗如有工艺缺陷可保修一年。 3 ★ to make sth certain to happen 使必然发生；确保 guaranteesth ◆ Tonight's victory guarantees the team's place in the final. 今晚的胜利确保这个队能进入决赛。 guaranteesb sth ◆ These days getting a degree doesn't guarantee you a job. 如今获得学位并不能保证你有工作。 4 guarantee(that)… to be certain that sth will happen 肯定…必然发生 ◆ You can guarantee (that) the children will start being naughty as soon as they have to go to bed. 可以肯定孩子一到上床睡觉时就不听话了。 5 to agree to be legally responsible for sth or for doing sth, especially for paying back money that sb else owes if they cannot pay it back themselves 承诺对…负法律责任；为…作担保 guaranteesth ◆ to guarantee a bank loan 为银行贷款作保 guaranteeto do sth ◆ to guarantee to pay sb's debts 为偿还某人的债务作保 guaranteethat… ◆ I guarantee that he will appear in court. 我保证他会出庭。 IDIOM be guaranˈteed to do sth to be certain to have a particular result 肯定会；必定会 ◆ If we try to keep it a secret, she's guaranteed to find out. 如果我们试图保密，她肯定会发现。 ◆ That kind of behaviour is guaranteed to make him angry. 那样的行为肯定会让他生气。 ☞synonyms at certain guar·an·tee / ˌɡærənˈtiː ;
