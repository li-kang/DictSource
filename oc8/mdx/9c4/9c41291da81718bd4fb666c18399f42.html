floun·der / ˈflaʊndə(r) ; NAmE ˈflaʊndər / verb , noun flounder flounders floundered floundering verb 1 [intransitive ,  transitive ] (+ speech) to struggle to know what to say or do or how to continue with sth 不知所措；挠头；支吾 ◆ His abrupt change of subject left her floundering helplessly. 他突然改变话题，使她茫然不知所措。 2 [intransitive ] to have a lot of problems and to be in danger of failing completely 困难重重；艰苦挣扎 ◆ At that time the industry was floundering. 那时这个行业举步维艰。 3 [intransitive ] (+ adv./prep.) to struggle to move or get somewhere in water, mud, etc. （在水、泥等中）挣扎 ◆ She was floundering around in the deep end of the swimming pool. 她在游泳池深水区挣扎着。 noun ( plural floun·der or floun·ders ) a small flat sea fish that is used for food 偏口鱼 floun·der / ˈflaʊndə(r) ; NAmE ˈflaʊndər /
