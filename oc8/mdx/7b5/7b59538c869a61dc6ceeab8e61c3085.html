preach / priːtʃ ; NAmE priːtʃ / verb 1 [intransitive ,  transitive ] to give a religious talk in a public place, especially in a church during a service 布道，讲道（尤指教堂中礼拜时） ◆ She preached to the congregation about forgiveness. 她向会众宣讲宽恕的道理。 preachsth ◆ The minister preached a sermon on the parable of the lost sheep. 牧师讲道时用了亡羊的比喻。 2 [transitive ,  intransitive ] to tell people about a particular religion, way of life, system, etc. in order to persuade them to accept it 宣传，宣扬，宣讲（教义、生活方式、体制等） preachsth ◆ to preach the word of God 传布上帝的道 ◆ He preached the virtues of capitalism to us. 他向我们宣扬资本主义的优点。 preach(about sth) ◆ She preached about the benefits of a healthy lifestyle. 她宣扬了健康生活的好处。 ☞ collocationsat religion 3 [intransitive ] ( disapproving) to give sb advice on moral standards, behaviour, etc, especially in a way that they find annoying or boring 说教 ◆ I'm sorry, I didn't mean to preach. 很抱歉，我并没有说教的意思。 preachat sb ◆ You're preaching at me again! 你又在对我说教了！ IDIOM preach to the conˈverted to speak to people in support of views that they already hold 向教徒宣教；教读书人写大字 ☞more at practise preach preaches preached preaching preach / priːtʃ ; NAmE priːtʃ /
