ap·pos·ition / ˌæpəˈzɪʃn ; NAmE ˌæpəˈzɪʃn / noun [uncountable ] ( grammar 语法 ) the use of a noun phrase immediately after another noun phrase that refers to the same person or thing 同位 ◆ In the phrase ‘Paris, the capital of France’, ‘the capital of France’ is in apposition to ‘Paris’. 在短语 Paris, the capital of France 中，the capital of France 是 Paris 的同位语。 apposition appositions ap·pos·ition / ˌæpəˈzɪʃn ; NAmE ˌæpəˈzɪʃn /
