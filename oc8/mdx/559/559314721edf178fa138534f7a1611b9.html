haem·or·rhage ( BrE) ( NAmE hem·or·rhage ) / ˈhemərɪdʒ ; NAmE ˈhemərɪdʒ / noun , verb haemorrhage haemorrhages haemorrhaged haemorrhaging noun 1 [countable ,  uncountable ] a medical condition in which there is severe loss of blood from inside a person's body 体内（大）出血 ◆ a massive brain/cerebral haemorrhage 重度脑溢血 ◆ He was checked for any signs of haemorrhage. 他接受检查，以确定是否有出血迹象。 2 [countable ,  usually singular ] haemorrhage(of sb/sth) a serious loss of people, money, etc. from a country, a group or an organization （人、资金等的）大量流失 ◆ Poor working conditions have led to a steady haemorrhage of qualified teachers from our schools. 工作条件欠佳导致各校大量合格教师不断流失。 verb 1 [intransitive ] to lose blood heavily, especially from the inside of the body; to have a haemorrhage （尤指体内）大出血 2 [transitive ] haemorrhagesb/sth to lose money or people in large amounts at a fast rate 大量快速流失（资金、人等） haem·or·rhage / ˈhemərɪdʒ ; NAmE ˈhemərɪdʒ /
