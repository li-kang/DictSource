wast·age / ˈweɪstɪdʒ ; NAmE ˈweɪstɪdʒ / noun 1 [uncountable ,  singular ] wastage(of sth) the fact of losing or destroying sth, especially because it has been used or dealt with carelessly 耗费；损耗；浪费 ◆ It was a new production technique aimed at minimizing wastage. 这是一项旨在使损耗减至最低的新生产技术。 2 [uncountable ] the amount of sth that is wasted 损耗量；耗费量；浪费量 ◆ There is little wastage from a lean cut of meat. 瘦肉的废弃部份很少。 3 [uncountable ] ( BrE) the loss of employees because they stop working or move to other jobs; the number of students who do not finish a particular course of study （雇员的）减员；（学生的）流失人数 ◆ Half of the posts will be lost through natural wastage. 有一半的职位通过自然减员将会丢失。 ◆ student wastage rates 学生流失率 wast·age / ˈweɪstɪdʒ ; NAmE ˈweɪstɪdʒ /
