mal·ice / ˈmælɪs ; NAmE ˈmælɪs / noun [uncountable ] a feeling of hatred for sb that causes a desire to harm them 恶意；怨恨 ◆ He sent the letter out of malice. 他出于恶意寄出了这封信。 ◆ She is entirely without malice. 她完全没有恶意。 ◆ He certainly bears you no malice (= does not want to harm you). 他对你一定不会有什么恶意。 IDIOM with ˌmalice aˈforethought ( law 律 ) with the deliberate intention of committing a crime or harming sb （犯罪或伤人）有恶意预谋的 mal·ice / ˈmælɪs ; NAmE ˈmælɪs /
