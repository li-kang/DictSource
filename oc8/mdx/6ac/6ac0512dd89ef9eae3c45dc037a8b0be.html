ac·credit / əˈkredɪt ; NAmE əˈkredɪt / verb 1 [usually passive ] ( formal) to believe that sb is responsible for doing or saying sth 把…归于；认为（某事为某人所说、所做） accreditsth to sb ◆ The discovery of distillation is usually accredited to the Arabs of the 11th century. 通常认为，蒸馏法是阿拉伯人在 11 世纪发明的。 accreditsb with sth ◆ The Arabs are usually accredited with the discovery of distillation. 通常认为，阿拉伯人发明了蒸馏法。 2 [usually passive ] accreditsb to … ( technical 术语 ) to choose sb for an official position, especially as an ambassador 委任，委派（某人为大使等） ◆ He was accredited to Madrid. 他被委任为驻马德里的大使。 3 accreditsth/sb to officially approve sth/sb as being of an accepted quality or standard 正式认可 ◆ Institutions that do not meet the standards will not be accredited for teacher training. 没有达标的大专院校不会获得教师培训的资格。 accredit accredits accredited accrediting ac·credit / əˈkredɪt ; NAmE əˈkredɪt /
