rugby / ˈrʌɡbi ; NAmE ˈrʌɡbi / ( sometimesRugby ) ( also ˌrugby ˈfootball ) noun [uncountable ] a game played by two teams of 13 or 15 players, using an ovalball which may be kicked or carried. Teams try to put the ball over the other team's line. 橄榄球运动 ORIGIN Named after Rugby school, where the game was first played. 以第一次开展橄榄球运动的拉格比学校命名。 rugby / ˈrʌɡbi ; NAmE ˈrʌɡbi /
