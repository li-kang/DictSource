zoom / zuːm ; NAmE zuːm / verb , noun zoom zooms zoomed zooming verb 1 [intransitive ] + adv./prep. ( informal) to move or go somewhere very fast 快速移动；迅速前往 SYN rush , whizz ◆ Traffic zoomed past us. 车辆从我们身边疾驰而过。 ◆ For five weeks they zoomed around Europe. 他们在欧洲各国马不停蹄地奔波了五个星期。 2 [intransitive ] zoom(up) (to…) ( informal) (of prices, costs, etc. 价格、费用等 ) to increase a lot quickly and suddenly 急剧增长；猛涨 ◆ House prices have zoomed up this year. 今年房屋价格飞涨。 PHRASAL VERB ˌzoom ˈin/ˈout (of a camera 摄影机或摄像机 ) to show the object that is being photographed from closer/further away, with the use of a zoom lens （用变焦距镜头）拉近，推远；使画面放大（或缩小） ◆ The camera zoomed in on the actor's face. 摄影机将演员的脸拉近了。 noun 1 [countable ] = zoom lens ◆ a zoom shot 用变焦距镜头拍的照片 2 [singular ] the sound of a vehicle moving very fast （车辆等）疾驰的声音 zoom / zuːm ; NAmE zuːm /
