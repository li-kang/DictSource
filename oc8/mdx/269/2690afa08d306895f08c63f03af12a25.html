hal·lu·cin·ation / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃn ; NAmE həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃn / noun 1 [countable ,  uncountable ] the fact of seeming to see or hear sb/sth that is not really there, especially because of illness or drugs 幻觉，幻视，幻听（尤指生病或毒品所致） ◆ to have hallucinations 产生幻觉 ◆ High temperatures can cause hallucination. 高烧可使人产生幻觉。 2 [countable ] something that is seen or heard when it is not really there 幻视（或幻听）到的东西；幻觉；幻象 ◆ Was the figure real or just a hallucination? 那个人影是真的呢，还是幻象？ HELP  Some speakers do not pronounce the ‘h’ at the beginning of hallucinationand use ‘an’ instead of ‘a’ before it.
