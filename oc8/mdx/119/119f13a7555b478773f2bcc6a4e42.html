in·er·tia / ɪˈnɜːʃə ; NAmE ɪˈnɜːrʃə / noun [uncountable ] 1 ( usually disapproving) lack of energy; lack of desire or ability to move or change 缺乏活力；惰性；保守 ◆ I can't seem to throw off this feeling of inertia. 我好像无法摆脱这种无力的感觉。 ◆ the forces of institutional inertia in the school system 学校体制内的惰性 2 ( physics 物 ) a property (= characteristic) of matter(= a substance) by which it stays still or, if moving, continues moving in a straight line unless it is acted on by a force outside itself 惯性 in·er·tia / ɪˈnɜːʃə ; NAmE ɪˈnɜːrʃə /
