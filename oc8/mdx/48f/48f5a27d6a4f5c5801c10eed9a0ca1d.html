an·aes·thet·ic ( especially US an·es·thet·ic ) / ˌænəsˈθetɪk ; NAmE ˌænəsˈθetɪk / noun , adjective anaesthetic anaesthetics noun [uncountable ,  countable ] a drug that makes a person or an animal unable to feel anything, especially pain, either in the whole body or in a part of the body 麻醉药；麻醉剂 ◆ How long will I be under anaesthetic? 我会给麻醉多长时间？ ◆ They gave him a general anaesthetic (= one that makes you become unconscious). 他们对他施行了全身麻醉。 ◆ (a) local anaesthetic (= one that affects only a part of the body) 局部麻醉 adjective [only before noun ] containing a substance that makes a person or an animal unable to feel pain in all or part of the body 麻醉的 ◆ an anaesthetic drug/spray 麻醉药╱喷剂 an·aes·thet·ic / ˌænəsˈθetɪk ; NAmE ˌænəsˈθetɪk /
