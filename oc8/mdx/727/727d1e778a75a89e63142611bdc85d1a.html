plead / pliːd ; NAmE pliːd / verb ( pleaded , pleaded NAmE also pled , pled / pled ; NAmE pled / ) 1 [intransitive ,  transitive ] to ask sb for sth in a very strong and serious way 乞求；恳求 SYN beg plead(with sb) (to do sth) ◆ She pleaded with him not to go. 她恳求他不要离开。 plead(with sb) (for sth) ◆ I was forced to plead for my child's life. 我被迫苦苦哀求给我的孩子一条生路。 ◆ pleading eyes 乞求的眼神 pleadto do sth ◆ He pleaded to be allowed to see his mother one more time. 他恳求准许他再看妈妈一眼。 + speech ◆ ‘Do something!’ she pleaded. “帮帮忙吧！”她央求道。 2 [intransitive ,  transitive ,  no passive ] to state in court that you are guilty or not guilty of a crime （在法庭）申辩，认罪，辩护 (+ adj.) ◆ to plead guilty/not guilty 认罪；不认罪 ◆ How do you plead? (= said by the judge at the start of the trial) 你有何辩护？ pleadsth ◆ He advised his client to plead insanity (= say that he/she was mentally ill and therefore not responsible for his/her actions). 他建议他的当事人以精神不正常作为辩护理由。 3 [transitive ] pleadsth to present a case to a court （向法庭）陈述案情 ◆ They hired a top lawyer to plead their case. 他们聘请了一位顶级律师帮他们陈述案情。 4 [transitive ,  no passive ] pleadsth (for sth) | pleadthat… to give sth as an explanation or excuse for sth 解释；推说；找借口 ◆ He pleaded family problems for his lack of concentration. 他解释说他不能集中精神是因为有家庭问题。 5 [transitive ,  intransitive ] to argue in support of sb/sth 为…辩护；声援；支持 pleadsth ◆ She appeared on television to plead the cause of political prisoners everywhere. 她出现在电视上为所有政治犯声援请命。 pleadfor sb/sth ◆ The United Nations has pleaded for a halt to the bombing. 联合国已呼吁停止轰炸。 plead pleads pleaded pled pleading plead / pliːd ; NAmE pliːd / pled / pled ; NAmE pled /
